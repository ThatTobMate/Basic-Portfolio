About

Below is a short timeline of my journey as a developer so far. I am really excited to get some commercial experience as a developer and work with a great team to help bring my skills forward and be a part of something that millions of people use. To download my CV click here.

  • June 2014

    The Beginning

    I started my journey as a developer in June 2014 by completing the 3 month web development immersive course at General Assembly. I quickly found just how exciting it is being faced with new challenges and trying to come up with solutions to fix problems, not just on your own but also within a great community.

  • September 2014

    Teaching Assistant

    On completion of the course I was asked to stay on at General Assembly as a Teaching Assistant. This was a really exciting opportunity for me to continue learning and to help other junior developers begin their journey.

  • January 2015

    Teaching Assistant Part 2

    I was asked to be a Teaching Assistant on the following course for the final time. Being a member of the instructional team was amazing, I learnt far more teaching people than I did as a student on the course. You become the students first point of call and to ensure I could help them I did extensive research on various topics and issues they had along the way.

  • April 2015

    Life After General Assembly

    I am currently looking for an exciting new role based in London. I want to develop my skills and continue learning by working on real world products!

Reader

RSS Feed Aggregator.

Reader is an RSS reading application where the user can log in with omniauth, subscribe to news feeds and read articles or bookmark them to read later. Created using Ruby on Rails, Javascript and the Feedjira gem for RSS parsing.

Clutch Gaming

ESports Gaming Website.

The next full web app that I am currenlty working on is called Clutch Gaming. Clutch Gaming is a site for competitive gaming where you can register your team to leagues and tournaments and challenge others in your favourite games across various consoles. I am using Rails API for the back end and AngularJS for the front end. I am managing the front end with tools such as Grunt and Bower. Currently creating MVP.
